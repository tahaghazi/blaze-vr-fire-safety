Why it works

Real instincts, built from safe repetition.

Children remember what they do far better than what they're told. BLAZE turns fire-safety drills into muscle memory through guided, immersive play — built on Unity, OpenXR, and a generative AI companion.

01

AI firefighter companion

An LLM-powered guide coaches every child in real time — proactively when they're stuck, and reactively on demand via an "Ask for Help" button. Responses arrive in under two seconds.

02

Learn the fire classes

Each scenario teaches the right tool for the job — foam, water, or CO₂ — plus oxygen-deprivation and evacuation tactics, all grounded in real fire-class theory.

03

Educator dashboard

A web dashboard tracks time-to-complete, error count, help requests, and star ratings per child — so teachers and parents see real improvement without touching the headset.

04

Plays anywhere

One Unity codebase runs on Meta Quest 2/3 and PC-tethered headsets (HTC Vive, Valve Index) via OpenXR — and falls back to keyboard, mouse, and tablet for the classroom.

The AI companion

A friendly firefighter, always by their side.

Instead of scripted hint trees, BLAZE's companion is powered by a live LLM — so it can answer almost any question a child asks within the scene, in three short, jargon-free, always-encouraging sentences.

Watches & guides

In proactive mode the companion steps in when a child idles, picks the wrong tool, or fails twice — offering a gentle nudge with no score penalty.

Answers on demand

Press "Ask for Help" and ask anything by voice or text. The reply streams back in under two seconds with lip-synced speech.

Celebrates wins

Every correct action earns a green checkmark, a happy sound, and verbal praise — keeping kids calm, confident, and engaged.

Inside the game

Six scenes. Six life-saving skills.

A single progressive level guides children from the simplest kitchen fire to a full school evacuation — each scene completable in 2–4 minutes, scaffolded to build on the last.

Scene 01 · Kitchen
Class B · Gas fire

Kitchen Gas Fire

Shut off the gas valve, grab the foam extinguisher, and aim at the base of the flame.

→ Cut the fuel before you fight the fire
Scene 02 · Kitchen
Class A · Contained

Burning Pot

No extinguisher needed — cover the pot with a lid and starve the fire of oxygen.

→ The fire triangle: fuel · heat · oxygen
Scene 03 · Living Room
Class A · Solid

Solid Material Fire

Pick the water extinguisher — not the CO₂ decoy — and put out a burning solid.

→ Match the extinguisher to the fire class
Scene 04 · Utility Room
Class E · Electrical

Electrical Fire

Drag a chair, climb to the breaker, cut the power, then use the CO₂ extinguisher.

→ Never use water — problem-solve first
Scene 05 · House
Structural · Evacuation

House Evacuation

Find the key, unlock the door, crawl below the smoke, and take the stairs — not the elevator.

→ Stay low · stairs over elevator
Scene 06 · School
Institutional · Capstone

School Evacuation

Pull the fire alarm, follow the EXIT signs, take the stairwell, and reach the assembly point.

→ The full evacuation protocol
